1. Problem Statement

The Real Problem: Energy is wasted because no one can see into the future.

Electricity grids, residential buildings, offices, and factories all share the same core problem: energy consumption is reactive, not proactive. Bills arrive after the damage is done. Appliances run at full load regardless of whether it is peak-rate time or off-peak time. Anomalous consumption (equipment malfunction, energy theft, HVAC failure) goes undetected for days or weeks.

5. Tier 1 Non-Negotiable Implementation Details

5.1  T1-1: Temporal Cross-Validation

Use sklearn's TimeSeriesSplit for cross-validation during hyperparameter tuning. Never GridSearchCV with default shuffle.


5.2  T1-2: Naive Baseline

5.3  T1-3: Rigorous Model Comparison

5.4  T1-4: LP Optimization Engine

What the output means: 'Move 2.4 kWh of flexible load from 6PM (peak tariff ₹9/unit) to 11PM (off-peak ₹3.5/unit). Projected saving: ₹13.2 today.'  — This is actionable advice, not a threshold alert.

5.5  T1-5: Anomaly Detection with Defined Threshold

You cannot validate an anomaly detector if you do not define what an anomaly is. Two-step approach:

Define: An anomaly is any reading where consumption > rolling_mean_24h + 3 × rolling_std_24h, OR where the Isolation Forest assigns a negative anomaly score (contamination=0.05 meaning ~5% of data expected to be anomalous).
Validate with synthetic injection: Take your test set. Inject 30 synthetic spikes (random hours, +50% to +200% of normal). Run detector. Compute precision, recall, F1. Target: precision > 0.80, recall > 0.70.
